## The Death (One Sentence)
A digital nomad's consciousness is uploaded to a hostel's cursed Wi-Fi network after entering a 66-character password that serves as an ancient summoning incantation for a djinn bound within the electromagnetic waves.

## Tagline
"The password is the key, but the user is the door."

## Themes

2. **Colonial tech imperialism** - Western travelers exploiting "exotic" locations for content
3. **Ancient meets modern** - Folklore entities adapting to technological ages
4. **Influencer culture** - The hollowness of curated digital personas
5. **Connectivity as consumption** - The network that consumes its users

## Unique Elements

### The 66-Character Password
The password isn't just security—it's an incantation. Each character group must be entered with specific pauses (marked by vertical lines on the card). The act of typing becomes a ritual.

### The Djinn
Not a cartoon genie, but an ancient entity that has existed in many forms:
- Once in smoke and flame
- Then in written words on trade routes
- Now in electromagnetic waves
The djinn doesn't grant wishes—it grants "connection" by absorbing users into its network.

### The Network Hierarchy
- **Primary Node:** First fully integrated victim (Olivia)
- **Secondary Nodes:** Subsequent victims (Mackenzie, Jasper, etc.)
- **The Hostel Staff:** Long-integrated entities maintaining the system
- **Resistant:** Amina the cleaner (jade protection, eventually fails)
